Showcasing museum activities with clear and engaging data visualizations

The Egyptian Museum of Turin, specialising in Egyptian archaeology and anthropology, commissioned us to design its first integrated report. The museum aimed to present a comprehensive 360-degree view of its activities, starting from KPIs that analyse and measure processes, narrated through clear and informative visualizations to guide the reading of the report.

CHALLENGE

To capture the full scope of the museum’s activities, we needed to combine quantitative KPIs with qualitative insights, creating a report that clearly communicates the processes and impact of the institution while highlighting the elements that define its value.

PROCESS

.1

Data analysis

.2

Design and iteration

.3

Report layout and delivery

Joining the project midway, I analyzed and refined existing visualizations for consistency and readability, while also creating new ones in line with the established visual guidelines. Close collaboration with the colleague responsible for the report layout ensured a balance between data complexity and available space, resulting in clear and legible representations.

SOLUTION

We focused on creating clear and impactful visualizations to effectively communicate the museum’s contributions. To enhance readability, legends were often integrated directly into the infographics, and a limited colour palette ensured simplicity and focus.
The report was divided into four sections, each represented by a specific colour from the museum’s brand identity. These colours were applied consistently across the graphic elements in each section to enhance clarity, guide navigation, and support understanding.

RESULT

The report gives users a clear view of the museum’s activities and their impact. Originally designed for digital use, it was so well-received that the client also requested a printed version.
