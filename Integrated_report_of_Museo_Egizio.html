DIGITAL AND PRINTED REPORT

Museo Egizio integrated report

During my experience at Accurat, I participated in the design of the report for the Museo Egizio. Considering I joined the project mid-way, my contributions focused on refining the graphic details and crafting a significant portion of the visualizations included in the report. I followed pre-established guidelines and adhered to the client's brand identity to ensure consistency and coherence. The report aimed to provide a comprehensive view of the institution's activities, encompassing aspects from research to social inclusion. This narrative was conveyed through impactful, accessible and easily readable visualizations.

The Egyptian Museum of Turin, specialising in Egyptian archaeology and anthropology, commissioned Accurat to design its first integrated report. The museum aimed to present a comprehensive 360-degree view of its activities, starting from KPIs that analyse and measure processes, narrated through clear and informative visualizations to guide the reading of the report.

GOAL

To create a document able to merge quantitative and qualitative aspects of the museum’s activities focusing on the elements that determine its value.

PROCESS

.1

Raw data in Excel

.2

Graphic experiments with visualizations

.3

Iteration based on feedback

.4

Report layout and delivery

Joining the project midway, I analyzed and refined approved visualizations for consistency and improved readability. Close collaboration with the colleague responsible for the report layout was crucial. For each visualization, I balanced data complexity with layout space to ensure clear and legible representations.
Initially, the section of the report containing the visualizations was planned to be green. However, based on client feedback, we changed it to blue, one of the most used colours of the client's brand identity.

OUTCOME

We divided the report into four sections, each marked by a specific color from the museum's brand identity. This use of color created coherent graphic elements and facilitated reading guidance. The final product features impactful, easily readable visualizations with legends incorporated directly into the infographics for better understanding.

RESULT

The report provides users with a clear understanding of the museum activities and their impact. Initially conceived for a digital format, the report was so well-received by the client that they requested a printed version as well.
