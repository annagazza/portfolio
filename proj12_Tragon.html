Tragon

The narrative The Dragon, written by Ray Bradbury, was translated into an illustrated Advent calendar for children. The trans-mediation of the original text occurs on both an analog level, the paper calendar itself, and on a digital level, through the visualization of the texts and the animations of the story using an application, which is an implementation of the physical product. The original themes of the ambivalence and the contrast between the contemporary age and the Middle Ages are stressed in different ways; starting from the integration with a digital medium that enriches the bidimensional images of the physical support, thanks to the animations and texts. The calendar narrates two parallel stories that cross in the middle: the moment when the two worlds meet is visually represented with a gradient that symbolizes their interaction and overlapping.
