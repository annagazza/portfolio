MICRO WEBSITE

Broken Promises

"Broken Promises" was the primary project I worked on during my collaboration with Accurat. TTP approached Accurat to create a digital experience based on their research, which aimed to expose Facebook’s policy violations between 2018 and 2021 and how the platform responded. Despite continuous assurances from Facebook spokespersons to address these issues, the annual number of violations remained consistent. We chose the glitch as a visual metaphor to symbolize these unfulfilled promises. I contributed to the concept development and the definition of the visual language, with my main responsibility being the design of the second section of the site.

The Tech Transparency Project (TTP) is a research initiative by the Campaign for Accountability, a nonprofit organization dedicated to exposing misconduct in public life, focusing on the power, influence, and effects of big tech companies. TTP created a dataset containing data on Facebook policy violations from 2018 to 2021 and how Meta responded to them, derived from news articles.

To design a narrative on a social relevant topic from raw data.

We analyzed the raw data provided by TTP to identify key insights and develop storytelling solutions. One of the first tasks was to categorize the violations into semantic clusters to reduce cognitive load for the user.
After considering different concepts, we chose a scrollytelling approach divided into two sections: the first on Facebook’s community guidelines violations, and the second on Meta's responses. Adopting a mobile-first approach ensured optimal user experience across all devices, recognizing that most users access the internet via mobile phones.

[A] The UX of the website

OUTCOME

Broken Promises is a digital experience that uses the glitch visual metaphor to narrate the insights derived from the data. The glitch symbolizes a system failure, highlighting that despite continuous policy violations and promises from Facebook, nothing changed during the considered period. The use of familiar graphic elements ties the narrative back to Facebook, creating a representation that feels both familiar and alien.
The first section provides context through text bubbles explaining the methodology. As the user scrolls, individual elements representing policy violations are revealed, each color-coded according to their category, culminating in the final visualization.
In the second section the focus shifts to how Facebook responded to the policy violations, revealing the infrequency of concrete actions. This section also includes a linguistic analysis showing the reuse of stock phrases over time and across different contexts.

[B] I designed this animation to ensure continuity with the first section while reinforcing the glitch theme to create a smooth user experience

[C] I designed the cards constructing a timeline to highlight the reuse of the same sentences by Facebook in various instances

RESULT

The project spotlighted Facebook's policy enforcement issues, enhancing public awareness and advocating for greater accountability in tech practices. It communicated the persistence of policy violations, fostering a deeper understanding among users.
