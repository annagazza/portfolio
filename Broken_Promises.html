Broken Promises

Raising awareness on Facebook policy violations through data storytelling

"Broken Promises" is a digital experience commissioned by the Tech Transparency Project (TTP) that reveals the persistence of Facebook’s policy violations between 2018 and 2021 and the platform’s limited responses. To illustrate the gap between repeated assurances and ongoing violations, the project uses the glitch as a visual metaphor for unfulfilled promises, while familiar graphic elements reference Facebook, creating a sense of both recognition and estrangement.

TTP is a research initiative by the Campaign for Accountability, a nonprofit organization based in the US and dedicated to exposing misconduct in public life, focusing on the power, influence, and effects of big tech companies.

CHALLENGE

Content moderation on social media is a socially relevant and often opaque process. To shed light on how Facebook policy violations are addressed and communicated to the public, TTP created a dataset from news articles documenting policy violations on the platform between 2018 and 2021, and Meta’s responses. This dataset provided the foundation for a data-driven narrative.

We analyzed the dataset, which was already organized into multiple semantic groups, and consolidated these into fewer, broader clusters, making it easier for users to navigate the content. This approach led us to design a two-part scrollytelling experience: the first section focuses on Facebook’s community guideline violations, while the second examines Meta’s responses.
We adopted a mobile-first approach, considering both the chosen medium and the fact that users today typically access the web on mobile devices, ensuring clarity and usability across multiple devices.

[A] The UX of the website

SOLUTION

We chose the glitch as a visual metaphor to highlight the persistence of Facebook policy violations despite repeated assurances, creating a narrative that feels both familiar and unsettling.
We structured the experience in two sections to reinforce the storytelling: the first presents the dataset and methodology, revealing individual policy violations and their categories to provide context, while the second focuses on Facebook’s responses, emphasizing the infrequency of concrete actions and the repetition of stock phrases over time.
We designed this division, along with color-coding and familiar graphic elements referencing Facebook, to guide users through the data, clarify complex information, and strengthen the project’s message about systemic issues.

[B] I designed this animation to ensure continuity with the first section while reinforcing the glitch theme to create a smooth user experience

[C] I designed the cards constructing a timeline to highlight the reuse of the same sentences by Facebook in various instances

RESULT

Through data-driven design and storytelling, the project has raised public awareness of Facebook’s policy enforcement practices and advocated for greater accountability in tech. By guiding users through the visual narrative, we fostered a deeper understanding of systemic issues and the broader implications of content moderation.
