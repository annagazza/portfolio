Invisible Trails

Exposing the hidden infrastructure behind apps through data storytelling

My thesis maps the complex digital infrastructure supporting free casual game apps on Android, aiming to demystify this complexity for users. By studying key players and their relationships, the research highlights a need for a user-friendly narrative, as users are often excluded by technical jargon meant for experts. "Invisible Trails" offers an alternative narrative to the typical app download experience, providing a space for discussion, awareness, and action. This project combines adversarial design and digital literacy to develop an educational tool that enhances users awareness, empowering them to make informed decisions.

Data analyzed

Game apps hide a complex infrastructure driven by data-centric business models designed to collect vast amounts of user data. This opaque ecosystem conceals multiple actors, preventing users from fully understanding the system. Trackers, which are third-party tools integrated into games, address economic and functional needs by simplifying development. They operate by collecting data and remain hidden from users, seamlessly blending into the game once integrated.

To narrow down the scope, I chose to focus on the top 100 freemium casual game apps available on the Google Play Store.

CHALLENGE

Despite their popularity, players often interact with apps without seeing the underlying data-driven infrastructure. The challenge was to translate this hidden system into a digital experience that exposes how data flows, allowing users to explore the system and reflect on the broader implications of data collection

I explored the topic through a combination of literature reviews and surveys with European gamers. The literature revealed a gap in projects addressing inexperienced users, while conversations with participants showed that, although they care about protecting their data, they often lack the knowledge to do so.

[A] The visualization shows the trackers headquarters, considering not only the actors but also their operating locations reflects the actual workings of a system that enables data collection, processing, and sharing worldwide

This insight shaped the decision to focus the project on helping inexperienced users navigate and reflect on these complex systems.

[B] Some responses to the question "How do you protect your privacy in relation to apps?"

From there, I developed a website designed to engage a broad audience and make the invisible workings of game apps tangible. The experience was optimized for mobile devices, and the visual language drew inspiration from familiar gaming interfaces, ensuring that exploration remained intuitive and engaging while highlighting the complexities of the system.

[C] The UX of the website

SOLUTION

Invisible Trails presents the findings of my research, revealing the hidden, data-driven infrastructure of game apps. The title plays on the dual meaning of “trail,” highlighting both the digital traces users leave behind and the journey these data undertake.

[D] The homepage offers an initial overview with all essential content structured to gradually provide necessary information for understanding the subsequent sections

The website was designed to guide users step by step: it first provides all the necessary context to understand the system and its actors, then invites exploration of a parallel app store experience to reveal what is actually downloaded with each game. To make an abstract system tangible, I used a travel metaphor: while users remain “still” playing, their data travels worldwide, illustrating the movement and reach of personal data.

[E] Continuing the narrative, a second visualization displays all the active trackers during the testing phase. Here, I used the patchwork metaphor to illustrate how these trackers integrate seamlessly into the game's code. Following this, the infrastructure's functioning is explained by showcasing the visual model that will be used in the specific cards for each app.

The interface uses a visual language inspired by familiar gaming elements, ensuring the experience feels intuitive and accessible. Methodologies and existing tools are embedded throughout, offering transparency and supporting further exploration.

[F] The thesis book details the state-of-the-art analysis, research, concept, and design choices made for "Invisible Trails

RESULT

With Invisible Trails, I aimed to raise awareness about an opaque phenomenon that often excludes users from the dialogue. Through communication design and data visualization, I created an interactive tool that makes this complex phenomenon tangible.
