INTERACTIVE REPORT

Making the invisible visible

An interactive exploration of visual content censorship

Making the Invisible Visible is an interactive report composed of eight visualizations that investigate online censorship from multiple perspectives. Through qualitative research and the development of original research protocols, we designed an experience that lets users explore how censorship practices operate and evolve across platforms and content types. Each visualization allows users to interact with the data, uncover patterns, and understand an otherwise opaque system, emphasizing transparency and accountability in content regulation.

Online censorship involves deliberately controlling or removing accessible content driven by governments and platforms aiming to regulate public domain content. This phenomenon gains significance as user engagement deepens, sparking debate about the reasons behind content censorship and the boundaries between appropriate and inappropriate content.

CHALLENGE

We needed to translate a complex, fragmented phenomenon into a structured digital report. This involved defining original research questions and protocols, and transforming heterogeneous data into visualizations that reveal patterns, mechanisms, and decision-making processes behind content censorship.

We approached online censorship by formulating eight research questions that explored the phenomenon from multiple perspectives, structured across three levels of analysis:

  1. The types of censored content and the different visual languages used
  2. The reactions that censorship produces, both in terms of offline movements and methods that users use to avoid it
  3. The workers behind it
My contribution focused on the censorship of locations, starting from the question: What kinds of places are censored on Google Maps, and how are they represented visually?

To investigate this, I built a dataset by collecting the first 50 relevant Google search results referencing censored locations through names or coordinates. These coordinates were then mapped onto Google Maps to create a visual archive, enabling an analysis of recurring censorship patterns and visual strategies.
To add a temporal dimension, I extended the research to Google Earth Pro, asking how the censorship of these locations evolved over time. By exploring satellite imagery from 2000 to 2019, I analyzed how different censorship techniques appeared, changed, or persisted, and how they related to specific types of places.

[A]A first draft of the visualization

This analysis informed the design of an interactive visualization structured around a navigable timeline. By interacting with the bars representing censored locations, users activate different moments in time, allowing them to observe how censorship methods shift and adapt, making an otherwise invisible process tangible and comparable.

SOLUTION

We designed a web-based experience combining eight interactive visualizations, explanatory texts, and methodological protocols. Users can explore patterns, mechanisms, and temporal changes in online censorship, gaining a clear understanding of how these practices operate across platforms and content types.

RESULT

Through this project, we emphasized the need for greater transparency in content regulation by making censorship practices and techniques visible over time. By combining interactive visualizations and structured research protocols, users can explore how censorship evolves and operates across different contexts
