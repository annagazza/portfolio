IoT

Dewo

"Dewo" is an IoT box designed for small-sized objects, aimed at improving children's autonomy by merging duty with fun. Targeting children aged 6-8, Dewo, thanks to its sensors, uses visual and sound stimuli to ensure clear and engaging interactions. The smart object works alongside an app that parents use to send tasks and confirm completion, while children interact with playful characters. My primary role involved designing the physical object, alongside contributing to interviews and shaping personas, UX scenarios, and the app's user experience.

Our task was to create an interactive product for children, exploring the typical design process for smart objects from the discovery phase to the design and delivery phase.

During a child’s day, there are a lot of important activities, which are often perceived as annoying, even if they are essential for their growth, such as tidying up. On the other hand, parents want their kids to be more autonomous.

GOAL

To design an IoT device that enhances children's engagement with daily tasks and facilitates parental involvement seamlessly and intuitively.

Our research focused on understanding children's brain development and activity preferences. Through desk research and hands-on activities with children aged 6-8, we found that boredom often arises from obligatory or solitary tasks, while interviews with parents revealed their perspectives and conflict resolution methods.

[A] Some of the cards we designed for the hands-on activities with children

Based on the interviews insights, we created personas and user journeys identifying “tidy up” as focus area.

[B] 2 of 4 personas created

[C] UX scenarios

We explored multiple concepts to address this issue, ultimately developing the idea of an animal-shaped box. This design encourages children to associate tidying up with caring for a pet, transforming a mundane chore into an engaging and interactive activity.

[D] Some sketches of the products

Next, we defined system components, evaluated feasibility, and established a visual language, leading to the development of new UX scenarios and an experience map for the application. The main challenge was to harmoniously integrate playfulness with responsibility education in a daily domestic context, ensuring both effectiveness and appeal for children.

[E] The experience map of the app

[F] Service architecture

I was responsible for prototyping the physical object. I designed a base shape that could be customized to resemble different animals and scaled to accommodate larger objects. This approach ensures versatility and adaptability, making the product functional and engaging for children.

[F] Render I did with 3DSMax

OUTCOME

Dewo is a box that, thanks to built-in sensors, helps children improve their autonomy by turning a boring task into a playful experience with sound and visual stimuli.

[G] Thanks to the weight sensor, Dewo perceives how many objects are inserted reacting every time by changing its eyes and saying short sentences or words that remind of food

[H] The smart object works alongside an app through which parents can send and confirm tasks

The app also features a child-friendly section based on a reward mechanism. Every time children complete a task, they earn points in the app, which they can use to unlock and customize characters.
Dewo makes tidying up fun and engaging, promoting independence and responsibility in children through interactive and rewarding experiences.

RESULT

Dewo's interactive design turns mundane chores into engaging experiences, fostering independence and responsibility. The project shows how technology can enhance learning processes and promote familial harmony.
