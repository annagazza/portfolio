Dewo

During a child’s day, there are a lot of important activities, which are often perceived as annoying, even if they are essential for their growth as tidying up. On the other hand, parents want their kids to be more autonomous. Dewo is a box for small-medium sized toys; that aims to improve the children's autonomy by bringing duty and fun together. Thanks to its animal shape, it helps kids to become more responsible: the action of tidying up is associated with the action of feeding an animal which is in the kids’ care. The smart object interacts with a mobile application to which it reacts with both visual and sound stimuli that together make the interaction clearer and more intuitive. The app is used by the parents to send the order and to confirm its completion, and by the children who can play with the characters. Website

[C] Thanks to the weight sensor, Dewo perceives how many objects are inserted reacting every time by changing its eyes and saying short sentences or words that remind of food

[D] The parent sends the task through the app to which Dewo reacts by saying “I am hungry” to make the kid understand that it is time to tidy up. The parents decide if the children have tidied up enough or not. If not, Dewo changes its eyes and says "I'm still hungry!"; when the children finish tidying up, Dewo reacts with heart eyes and saying “I’m full, thank you!” to let them know that they have finished

[E] Every time the children complete a task, they earn some points on the mobile application that allow them to unlock and customize the animals on the app

[F] Dewo is thought to be scalable to different sizes and can take several animal shapes, keeping the same form of the body to make all the products consistent
